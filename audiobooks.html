Audiobooks

The Hunger Games

In the ashes of a fallen North America, the Capitol keeps control through a brutal televised competition known as the Hunger Games. Each district must sacrifice one boy and one girl to fight to the death, and when sixteen-year-old Katniss Everdeen volunteers to save her sister, she becomes a symbol of defiance she never intended to be. This haunting audiobook captures every heartbeat of fear, courage, and rebellion in vivid, cinematic detail.

Catching Fire

After surviving the Hunger Games, Katniss and Peeta return home as victors—but their defiance has sparked something dangerous. The Capitol wants revenge, and rebellion is spreading through the districts. As Katniss is thrust back into the arena, she must face impossible choices about love, loyalty, and survival. The second audiobook intensifies the suspense, blending political intrigue, action, and raw human emotion in Suzanne Collins’s signature style.

Mockingjay

The revolution has begun, and the world burns around Katniss Everdeen. Haunted by the past and torn between duty and heart, she becomes the reluctant face of a movement that could either free Panem—or destroy it entirely. As alliances fracture and the truth of war unfolds, this powerful final audiobook delivers a breathtaking and devastating conclusion to the series, exploring sacrifice, justice, and the fragile cost of hope.

Info

Suzanne Collins is an American author and screenwriter best known for The Hunger Games trilogy — a landmark work in modern literature that has sold millions of copies worldwide and inspired an award-winning film franchise. Born in Hartford, Connecticut, Collins began her career in television, writing for several Nickelodeon shows before transitioning to novels. Her background in screenwriting is evident in her vivid world-building, cinematic pacing, and sharp dialogue that bring her stories to life across mediums. Through The Hunger Games, Catching Fire, and Mockingjay, Collins explores the intersections of power, media, survival, and resistance. Her work confronts readers with difficult moral questions about violence, spectacle, and the cost of freedom — while remaining deeply human at its core. The trilogy’s central heroine, Katniss Everdeen, has become a cultural icon and symbol of defiance against oppression, resonating with readers of all ages across the world.
